Addition
  • The process of adding a number to another number.
  • The total of the 2 or more numbers added together is called the sum.
  • Represented by the plus (+) sign.
  • Ex: 2+3=5
Subtraction
  • The process of taking an amount from a number.
  • Represented by the minus (-) sign.
  • Ex: 5-3=2
Multiplication
  • The process of multiplying a number by another number.
  • The total of 2 or more numbers multiplied together is called the product.
  • Represented by the times (×) sign.
  • Ex: 2×3=6
Division
  • The process of dividing, or separating a number into equal parts.
  • The total of dividing a number by a number is called the quotient.
  • Represented by the division (÷) sign.
  • The amount left over is called a remainder (R).
  • Ex: 6÷3=2
Exponent
  • A second number placed above and to the right of another number.
  • Shows how many times a number should be multiplied by itself.
  • Ex: 5^2=25
Order of Operations
  • A rule which states which mathematic skill should be done first in an equation.
  • Involves the use of addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, exponents, and the parentheses () symbol.
  • “PEMDAS”, or “Please Excuse My Dear Aunt Sally” is a common acronym used to remember what skill to perform first.
  • P: Parentheses, E: Exponent, M: Multiplication, D: Division, A: Addition, and S: Subtraction
  • The M & D can be switched, depending on which skill comes first reading the equation from left to right. Same thing applies to the A & S.
