Physical vs. Chemical Changes
  • A physical change doesn't change what the substance is
  • A chemical change causes a chemical reaction, a new substance is formed
  • Example: if a piece of paper is cut up into small pieces it's still paper. This would be a physical change in the shape and size of the paper.
  • Example: if the paper is burned, it is broken up into different substances that are not paper.
    • Constellations and Earth Movements
  • A constellation is a group of stars that are considered to form imaginary outlines or meaningful patterns on the celestial sphere
  • The Earth rotates on its axis from west east. The axis is an imaginary line that goes through the north and south poles
  • The Earth's rotation is completed in 24 hours
    • The Three Types of Rock Formation
  • Sedimentary rocks are formed through the gradual accumulation of sediments, for example, sand on a beach or mud on a river bed
  • Igneous rocks are rocks which have crystallized from a melt or magma. As it cools and various rock types will form through a process known as fractional crystallization.
  • Metamorphic rocks are rocks which once existed as igneous or sedimentary rocks but have been subjected to varying degrees of pressure and heat of the Earth's crust.
