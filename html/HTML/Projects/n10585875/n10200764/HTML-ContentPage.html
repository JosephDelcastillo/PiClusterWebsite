Stages of the Water Cycle
Evaporation: The process of liquid water absorbing enough energy to become a gas.

How does it work: Evaporation happens when atoms or molecules escape from the liquid and turn into a vapor.

  • Evaporation happens more quickly at higher temperatures then lower temperatures.

  • The rate of Evaporation depends on the liquid's exposed surface area , the humidity of the surroundings area, wind , and temperature.

Examples of Evaporation include:

  • Seeing the morning dew on the grass in the morning, but the grass is dry in the afternoon.

  • A puddle in the street getting smaller and smaller everyday.

  • Washing a pan and leaving it to dry on the rack.
Condensation: Water vapor in the air goes from a gas, back into a liquid form.

How does it work:Occurs in the atmosphere when warm air rises, cools and looses its capacity to hold water vapor, forming cloud droplets.

  • The creation of clouds, is a result of the process of condensation.

  • The primary route for water to return to the Earth's surface within the water cycle.

  • When molecules lose energy, they slow down and begin to form a liquid.

Examples of Condensation include:

  • Having a cold soda on a hot day, the can "sweats." Water molecules in the air as a vapor hit the cold surface of the can and turn into liquid water.

  • Your mirror in the bathroom becoming foggy during a shower because warmer water vapor in the air hits the cooler surface of the mirror.
Precipitation: Precipitation takes place when water condensation becomes plenty of, that water droplets are heavy enough to fall back to the surface of the Earth.
  • It can be liquid or solid

  • Plants need water in order to survive, they receive much of this water from rain.

Examples of Precipitation include:

  • Rain

  • Sleet

  • Snow

  • Hail
