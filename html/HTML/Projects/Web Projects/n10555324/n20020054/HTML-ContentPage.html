Matter
-Matter is anything that has mass and takes up space.
-Matter has three different states: Liquid, Gas, and Solid
-Matter is made up of atoms which are the basic unit in science
Properties of Matter
-The three properties for matter: Volume, Shape, and Mass
-Volume is the amount that a substance or object can take up
-Mass is the amount of atoms in the object or substance
-Shape is the form that an object or substance has

Solids
-Solids are a substance or object that has a more controlled figure
-Solids have a definite shape
-Solids have a definite volume
-Solids have a definite mass

Liquids
-Liquids is a substance that flows
-Liquids have no permanent shape but when put in a container it takes the shape of the container
-Liquids have volume because they take up space when put into containers or other objects
-Liquids also have mass when placed into containers/other objects

Gases
-Gases are air-like substances that expand freely within any open space
-Gases do not have a definite shape
-Gases do not have a definite mass
-Gases do not have a definite volume
