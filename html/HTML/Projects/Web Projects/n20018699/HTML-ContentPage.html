Density Math
Density can be calculated by dividing the mass by the volume. Mass is measured in grams (g), and volume is measured in centimeters cubed (cm3). Due to mass being divided by volume equaling density, the unit for density is grams per centimeters cubed, or g/cm3.
If an object is less dense than water, meaning the number for the density is lower, that object can float. See if the following objects can float given their mass and volume. (Density of water: 1g/cm3
  • Pyrite (Fool's Gold): 40g; 8cm3
  • Boat: 1,898,000g; 6,871,554.77cm3
  • Paper Clip: 1g; 0.13cm3
