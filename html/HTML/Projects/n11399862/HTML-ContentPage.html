From a Soldier's Perspective

Unlike any other war, the Vietnam War was the longest war fought, lasting up 20 years. Many soldiers were drafted & fought in the war. An issue with this war is it was difficult to tell the difference between friend or enemy. On ground, soldiers had to always be alert because of hidden traps were scattered everywhere. There could be a surprise attack at any moment. 58,220 U.S. soldiers died in Vietnam.


  • Novemeber 1, 1975: North Vietnam invades South Vietnam and take control of the country.

  • March 8, 1965: U.S. troops enter Vietnam.

  • April 30, 1975: Saigon, the capital of South Vietnam, fell to the communists. Thus, ending the war.
