Let's Get to the Basics!

Mammals

There is a lot of stuff that makes a mammal a mammal. One thing an animal must have is the ability to make milk for their babies. Next, all mammals are warm blooded and have hair or fur. Most mammals have teeth, not including the ant eater because it doesn't have teeth. FUN FACT: Humans are mammals!

Birds

Birds are feathered, warm blooded creatures. there is over 9,000 types o f birds in the world. All birds of flight have hollow bones which allow them to fly. The ostrich, penguin, and dodo bird do not have the ability to fly. The ostrich is the largest bird in the world. FUN FACT: the only bird in the world without wings is called a kiwi.

Reptiles

Reptiles are cold-blooded animals that have dry, hard, scaled skin. Most reptiles lay shelled eggs. Since they are cold blooded they do not have a certain body heat. Reptiles need regular sunlight to keep their body warm. FUN FACT: The biggest reptile is the salt water crocodile reaching 12 feet long.

Amphibians

Amphibians earned their name because they live their first half of life in water and their final half on land.when they first hatch, amphibians are born with gills in order for them to swim and breathe water like fish do. Amphibians are cold blooded and transform from their larvae stage as time goes on. FUN FACT: getting warts from touching a frog or toad is a MYTH!
